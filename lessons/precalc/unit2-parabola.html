Unit 2: Parabola and Ellipse

Learning Objectives

  • Derive standard equations for parabola and ellipse.
  • Identify focus, directrix, vertices, major/minor axis.
  • Graph and solve situational problems.

Parabola

A parabola is the set of points equidistant from focus and directrix. Eccentricity e = 1.

Standard forms:

  • Vertical: (x - h)² = 4p(y - k)
  • Horizontal: (y - k)² = 4p(x - h)

Focus: (h, k + p) or (h + p, k); Directrix opposite.

Ellipse

Sum of distances to two foci is constant. Eccentricity 0 < e < 1.

Standard: (x - h)²/a² + (y - k)²/b² = 1 (a > b, horizontal).

c² = a² - b², foci at (±c, k).

Examples

Parabola: (x - 1)² = 8(y + 2) → p = 2, focus (1, 0).

Ellipse: x²/25 + y²/16 = 1 → a=5, b=4, c=3, foci (±3, 0).

Interesting Facts

  • Parabolas reflect light/sound to focus (satellite dishes, headlights).
  • Ellipses: Planetary orbits (Kepler's 1st law), whispering galleries.
