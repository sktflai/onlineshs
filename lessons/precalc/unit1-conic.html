Unit 1: Introduction to Conic Sections and the Circle

Learning Objectives

  • Identify and illustrate the four conic sections.
  • Derive the standard equation of a circle.
  • Determine the center and radius from the equation.
  • Graph circles and solve related problems.

Detailed Explanation

Conic sections are curves obtained by intersecting a plane with a double cone. The four types are:

  • Circle: Plane perpendicular to the cone axis.
  • Ellipse: Plane tilted but not parallel to generator.
  • Parabola: Plane parallel to generator.
  • Hyperbola: Plane steeper than generator.

The standard equation of a circle with center (h, k) and radius r is:

(x - h)² + (y - k)² = r²

Examples

Example 1: Find the equation of a circle with center (-2, 3) and radius 7.

Solution: (x + 2)² + (y - 3)² = 49

Example 2: Find center and radius of x² + y² + 8x - 6y + 16 = 0.

Complete the square: (x + 4)² + (y - 3)² = 9 → Center (-4, 3), radius 3.

Interesting Facts

  • A circle has eccentricity e = 0 (most "round" conic).
  • Real-life uses: Wheels, planetary orbits (approximately circular), optical lenses.
