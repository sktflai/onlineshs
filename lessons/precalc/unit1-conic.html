Introduction to Conic Sections

Conic sections are curves obtained by intersecting a plane with a double-napped cone. The four types are circle, ellipse, parabola, and hyperbola, depending on the plane's angle.

1. The Circle

Standard Equation: (x − h)² + (y − k)² = r²
Center: (h, k)    Radius: r

Example: (x + 1)² + (y − 4)² = 25 → Center: (−1, 4), Radius: 5

2. The Ellipse

Standard Equation (horizontal major axis):
\frac{(x - h)^2}{a^2} + \frac{(y - k)^2}{b^2} = 1    (a > b)
Center: (h, k)    Vertices: (±a from center along major axis)

The sum of distances from any point on the ellipse to the two foci is constant.
