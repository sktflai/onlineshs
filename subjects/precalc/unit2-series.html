Unit 2: Series and Mathematical Induction

Grade 11 STEM – DepEd Philippines Curriculum

Learning Objectives:

  • Define and illustrate sequences and series
  • Determine arithmetic, geometric, harmonic sequences and their sums
  • Apply the binomial theorem
  • Prove statements using mathematical induction

1. Sequences and Series

A sequence is an ordered list of numbers following a pattern. A series is the sum of the terms of a sequence.

2. Arithmetic Sequence

An arithmetic sequence has a constant difference (common difference d) between terms.

General term: a_n = a_1 + (n-1)d

Sum of first n terms: S_n = n/2 (a_1 + a_n) or S_n = n/2 [2a_1 + (n-1)d]

Example: Sequence: 3, 7, 11, 15, ...
a_1 = 3, d = 4
a_10 = 3 + (10-1)×4 = 39
S_10 = 10/2 × (3 + 39) = 210

3. Geometric Sequence

A geometric sequence has a constant ratio (common ratio r) between terms.

General term: a_n = a_1 × r^(n-1)

Sum of first n terms: S_n = a_1 (1 - r^n)/(1 - r) (r ≠ 1)

Infinite sum (if |r| < 1): S = a_1 / (1 - r)

Example: Sequence: 2, 6, 18, 54, ...
a_1 = 2, r = 3
a_8 = 2 × 3^7 = 4374
S_8 = 2 (1 - 3^8)/(1 - 3) = 6560

Helpful Video: Geometric Sequences & Series - Khan Academy

4. Harmonic Sequence

A harmonic sequence is the reciprocal of an arithmetic sequence.

Example: 1, 1/2, 1/3, 1/4, ...

5. Binomial Theorem

The binomial theorem gives the expansion of (a + b)^n.

(a + b)^n = Σ C(n,k) a^(n-k) b^k (k from 0 to n)

C(n,k) are binomial coefficients, often from Pascal's Triangle.

Example: (x + 2)^4 = x^4 + 8x^3 + 24x^2 + 32x + 16

Helpful Video: Binomial Theorem - Khan Academy

6. Mathematical Induction

Mathematical induction proves statements for all natural numbers.

Steps:

  1. Base Case: Prove true for n=1 (or smallest value)
  2. Inductive Step: Assume true for n=k, then prove true for n=k+1

Example: Prove sum of first n odd numbers = n²
Base: n=1 → 1=1²
Assume for k: 1+3+...+(2k-1)=k²
For k+1: + (2(k+1)-1) = k² + (2k+1) = (k+1)²
